Analytical Edge: Social Media & Forum Intelligence via Computer

Computer gathered practitioner discussions from LinkedIn, Reddit, and pharma IT forums, cross-referenced with published industry research. The consensus from real users: enterprise migrations in regulated environments are painfully slow. Dual systems must run in parallel for compliance. With 300,000+ pharmaceutical product records, every data point must be validated, every workflow re-tested. Migrations planned for months stretch into years.

This is exactly what Novartis faces. Their Salesforce rollout is a 5-year global implementation. The market treated this as an instant loss for Veeva — but signing a contract is not the same as going live. Industry data confirms: only 8% of SAP enterprise migrations finish on schedule, and 60%+ exceed budget.

Veeva's moat isn't just financial switching costs — it's 21 CFR Part 11 compliance (electronic records/signatures), ICH E6 R3 (GCP modernization, finalized 2025), and GxP validation that adds 12–18 months and 25–40% of project cost to any migration. Generalist platforms cannot replicate this without years of domain-specific engineering.
